About Me

I hold a degree in law and I have a background in business development with 20 years of experience. I worked for a well-known company in Estonia for a bit over 20 years, helping them to expand their business and grow. My main focus was developing their webshop.

I held various positions in that company which made it possible for me to grow in my job and climb up the career ladder. At some point I felt I had reached the top and needed a career change. After a long consideration I decided to quit my job in May 2020 and make a transition into a new career as a software developer.

I joined Treehouse - an online learning platform for coding - in May 2020. I hopped on board with their Python track which I successfully completed. As I had found my new passion I wished to take my studies further and enrolled in a 8-month Python bootcamp in September 2020. Alongside my bootcamp I also take the course "CS50's Web Programming with Python and JavaScript" provided by Harvard University and study front end (HTML, CSS, JavaScript) at Treehouse.

#100DaysOfCode Challenge

When I joined Treehouse in May 2020 they were promoting this #100DaysOfCode challenge which I had never heard about. After doing some research I decided to join with the challenge. The basic idea is to code 100 days straight and let others know about your progress on social media. I chose Twitter to be that channel I would use to tweet about my progress. I did code and tweeted every day and also tagged Treehouse in my tweets so they could cheer me on.

It was truly an amazing experience and showed me how much you can achieve by being consistent. Consistency is a powerful tool if you wish to be really good at something. After completing the challenge (on August 31th) I was awarded with a nice badge from Treehouse (please see the image below). I decided to go for a 2nd round of #100DaysOfCode in September and completed it on December 18th.

After completing my first round of the #100DaysOfCode challenge I reflected back on the experience and posted some thoughts on this. You can read about it here.

Mentoring

I remember very well when I got started with coding and got stuck doing different challenges at Treehouse. It was good to know that I could always reach out to their supportive online community. At some point I started to feel really comfortable with Python and decided it was time I helped out those students who were new to coding and needed assistance. So this is why I volunteer in the Treehouse Community every once in a while. Overall I have helped out a bit over 50 students there. It feels so rewarding to get positive feedback from the students I've helped!

In January 2021 I had the opportunity to volunteer as a mentor in a few online Python seminars organized by the CS50 staff at Harvard University. This was a very unique experience as I did it in real-time when those seminars took place. I had to be quick catching questions from the students and in answering them as there were a lot of students all around the world attending. It was quite an adrenaline rush! Everyone should get out of their comfort zone every once in a while and take on new challenges.
